# Retention Signal Desk

Retention Signal Desk is an AI-powered employee retention and talent re-engagement platform for employers. It helps companies detect which employees may be at risk of leaving before they resign, while also helping hiring managers rediscover strong past candidates, former employees, and ex-interns when new roles open.

## One-liner

Detect who is about to quit before they do, and automatically match open roles to your best past candidates.

## Problem

Most companies miss early warning signs from their own talent. A valuable employee may take on heavier responsibilities without promotion, receive less recognition, or show subtle changes in manager feedback over time. However, these signals are usually scattered across spreadsheets, reviews, salary records, and informal comments, so managers only notice the problem when the resignation letter arrives.

At the same time, employers often restart hiring from zero, even though they already have access to people who know the company and were previously vetted, including former employees, past candidates, and former interns.

These two issues are connected. They both happen because employers do not have a system that can read and act on the talent signals they already have.

## Solution

Retention Signal Desk combines two connected AI modules built on a shared Talent Pool Database.

### Career Intelligence Engine

The Career Intelligence Engine analyzes current employees using signals such as workload, project completion, manager feedback, recognition trends, and promotion timeline. It produces:

- A retention risk score from 0 to 100
- A risk level of Low, Medium, or High
- The top reasons behind the risk
- A short prediction of what may happen next
- Suggested manager actions with specific, practical language

Instead of giving a vague black-box score, the system explains each result with concrete evidence, such as delayed promotion, increased workload, or a drop in recognition. This helps managers walk into retention conversations with real context instead of guesswork.

### Opportunity Matching Engine

When a new job opening is created, the Opportunity Matching Engine searches across current employees, former employees, past candidates, and former interns. It uses semantic matching rather than simple keyword search, so candidates with similar skills described in different ways can still be found.

Each candidate is ranked based on:

- Skill fit
- Experience match
- Cultural fit
- Availability likelihood

The system also generates personalized outreach messages based on the candidate's past relationship with the company. For example, a former intern's message can reference their actual internship project, while a past candidate's message can acknowledge that the timing was not right before but the new role is a better fit.

## Key Product Linkage

The strongest part of Retention Signal Desk is the link between retention and hiring. If a manager sees that an employee is high-risk, they can immediately trigger a successor search based on that employee's current role.

This allows the company to respond in two ways at once: try to retain the employee while preparing a backup talent pipeline. The product does not treat retention and hiring as separate workflows. It connects them into one proactive talent strategy.

## AI Usage

AI is central to the product in three areas.

First, the system uses AI to detect retention risk from unstructured manager feedback. A comment such as "she has been solid but seems checked out lately" may not trigger a normal keyword rule, but an LLM can understand the sentiment and connect it with other signals such as workload or delayed promotion.

Second, the system uses embeddings and semantic search to match skills across different wording. For example, "customer discovery interviews" and "UX research" may describe similar capabilities even though the keywords are different.

Third, the system uses an LLM to generate personalized outreach messages at scale. This makes re-engagement warmer and more specific than a generic recruitment email.

Without AI, the product would be a basic dashboard and search tool. AI makes it predictive, explainable, specific, and actionable.

## Demo Data Strategy

The 28-day demo will use synthetic but realistic data generated by a deterministic Python script with a fixed random seed. This makes the demo reproducible for judges.

The dataset includes:

- 40 current employees with workload, salary, promotion, and manager feedback history
- 20 former employees with exit reasons and recontact flags
- 25 past candidates with interview feedback and rejection reasons
- 15 former interns with performance scores and graduation dates
- 10 open job opportunities with required skills and cultural preferences

The synthetic data is designed to show realistic variation, including high-risk employees, borderline cases, and low-risk employees.

## Why Talentbank

Retention Signal Desk directly addresses two Talentbank employer-side challenge areas: Talent Retention Signals and Talent Re-Engagement.

The product fits Talentbank because it can use data that Talentbank's employer network already generates, including job postings, interview records, candidate profiles, and employer feedback. This reduces the cold-start problem and makes adoption easier for companies.

Employers do not need to change their existing workflows. The system reads data from what they already do and adds an intelligence layer on top. This makes Retention Signal Desk practical as a value-added feature for Talentbank's existing employer network.
